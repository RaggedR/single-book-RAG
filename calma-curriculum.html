Design Principles

  • Start with easy, continuous sounds (/m/, /n/, /s/) — easy to hear and say
  • Progress to stops and fricatives (/p/, /b/, /t/, /k/, /f/)
  • Finish with complex sounds (/sh/, /ch/, /th/, /r/, /l/)
  • Each sound is taught through a 5-step cycle

Teaching Cycle

1
Listen — hear the sound in isolation
2
Watch — see mouth shape & pronunciation
3
Repeat — say the sound aloud
4
Label — recognise the phoneme symbol (/s/)
5
Match — connect to the written letter (s)

Exercise 3.4
Combination Discovery

Affricate: /tʃ/ (ch)

"This sound is special — it starts like /t/ (a stop) and ends like /ʃ/ (a slide). Listen: 'ch'. It's quick — stop then slide!"

Isolation: "Is this /tʃ/ or /ʃ/?" (8 trials)
Production: "Say 'ch' three times. Make sure it starts with a little pop."

Exercise 3.5
Tactile + Visual

The /θ/ and /ð/ Pair (th)

"Stick your tongue out a little between your teeth. Now blow air over it — that's /θ/ (like 'think'). Now add your voice — that's /ð/ (like 'this')."

Mirror/camera exercise: "Can you see your tongue? Good!"

8 discrimination trials, /θ/ vs. /ð/

These are the hardest fricatives. Allow more repetition — don't gate progress on perfection here.

Stage 4

Liquids & Harder Consonants

Sessions 17–20
SoundExampleLabelNotes
/r/"rrr"/r/Tongue slightly back, rolled or bunched
/l/"lll"/l/Tongue tip at alveolar ridge
/j/"yuh"/j/Glide consonant
Exercise 4.1
Approximation + Patience

/r/ Introduction

"This is one of the trickiest sounds! Pull your tongue slightly back and make a sound like a quiet engine: 'rrr'."

1. Listen to /r/ modeled 5 times (slow, exaggerated)
2. 3-way discrimination: /r/ vs. /l/ vs. /w/ (8 trials)

/r/ may take many sessions. Track attempts and offer encouragement without gating.

Exercise 4.2
Tongue Placement Focus

/l/ Introduction

"Touch the bumpy ridge behind your top teeth with your tongue tip. Now let sound come out around the sides — 'lll'."

Discrimination from /r/ and /n/ (8 trials) + production practice

Exercise 4.3
Glide Discovery

/j/ (y) Introduction

"Start saying 'eee' and then open your mouth — you just made /j/! That's the sound at the start of 'yes'."

Discrimination from /l/ — 6 trials

Progression

Assessment & Adaptive Logic

CriterionThresholdAction
Sound discrimination ≥ 80% Unlock next stage
Sound-to-letter ≥ 75% Unlock letter exercises
Production (teacher) Acceptable attempt Don't gate on production alone
CVC blending 3/5 words Unlock CCVC/CVCC
Retention (24h later) ≥ 70% Proceed; else revisit

Spaced Repetition

Sounds from earlier stages appear in 20% of exercises at later stages to prevent decay.

Adaptive Difficulty

If a learner gets 3 wrong in a row on a specific sound pair (e.g., /s/ vs. /ʃ/), insert targeted remediation exercises for that pair before continuing.
